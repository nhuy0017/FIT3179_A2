Indigenous Language Vitality and Population Insights

This collection of data has been provided by the Australian Bureau of Statistics. Key data includes the distribution of Indigenous languages, assistance needs, labour force participation, careers and educations, sourced from Indigenous status datasets, housing, education, and language proficiency tables. This visualization shows the distribution of Indigenous languages, their vitality, and related socio-economic factors across Australia. It visualizes key aspects like population concentration, language proficiency, labour force status and highest completed education, using a mix of maps, network diagrams, and other charts to provide insights into the cultural and demographic landscape of Australia's Indigenous communities.

Indigenous overview

Using dot maps, the visualisation provides information about the Indigenous languages in Australia. It showcases the traditional territories of various Indigenous groups and the population density of language speakers within these regions.
Press the button to change the view perspective. Hover over the dots to reveal tooltips with additional details.

Population Distribution

This visualization depicts the total number of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people across different states in Australia. Using a Choropleth Map, it visually represents the population distribution, allowing for a clear understanding of the demographic landscape. Each state is colored based on the total Indigenous population, with deeper shades indicating higher numbers. This visualization aims to highlight the diversity and distribution of Indigenous populations, fostering awareness and understanding of their significance within the broader Australian context.
Hover over the grids to reveal tooltips with additional details.

Core Activities Need for Assistance by Age(s)

This pie chart visualization displays the proportions of core assistance needs among Indigenous peoples. The chart categorizes individuals based on their assistance requirements: those who need support for core activities, those who require assistance but do not have Indigenous status, and those who do not require assistance. Providing insights into the varying levels of support needed within the Indigenous population.
Hover over the pies to reveal tooltips with additional details.

Labour Force Status

This heat map visualization highlights the relationship between labour force status and age groups among Indigenous Australians. It categorizes individuals based on four key labour force statuses: Employed, Unemployed, Not in the Labour Force, and Labour Force Status Not Stated. Each cell in the heat map represents the count of persons for a specific age group and labour force status,providing an at-a-glance understanding of how labour force participation varies across different age ranges.
Hover over the grid to reveal tooltips with additional details.

Careers: Occupations and Industries

These bar charts provide a breakdown of Australian Indigenous and Torres Strait Islander people by sex, across different industries and occupations. They highlight the workforce participation of Indigenous men and women, revealing gender representation trends across various sectors. By comparing male and female employment in different industries, the charts help identify workforce patterns, gender disparities, and areas for promoting more balanced employment opportunities for Indigenous communities.
Hover over the bars to reveal tooltips with additional details.

Education of Indigenous people

These radar charts display the highest school years completed by Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people, providing a clear comparison of educational attainment within these two groups. Each chart maps the distribution of school completion levels, from early school years to the highest levels of secondary education. By examining these charts, we can gain insights into the education patterns and achievements of Indigenous communities, highlighting differences or similarities in school completion rates and identifying potential areas where educational support and development may be needed to improve outcomes for both groups.
Press the button to change the view perspective. Hover over the dots to reveal tooltips with additional details.
